# VISION — LeetWrite AI

## Why LeetWrite AI Exists

Every developer who solves a hard problem has a small moment of pride right after the "Accepted" mark appears. That moment is the best possible time to capture a clear explanation of what they just did — and it's exactly the moment most people abandon, because writing it up well takes almost as long as solving it did.

LeetWrite AI exists to close that gap. Not to write the solution. Not to think for the user. Just to remove the tax on sharing good work.

---

## What Problem It Solves

The problem isn't a lack of AI access — everyone already has ChatGPT or Gemini open in another tab. The problem is friction: copying the problem, copying the code, writing a prompt, reformatting the response, checking the complexity analysis, fixing the markdown. That's ten small steps standing between a solved problem and a published one.

LeetWrite AI solves the *workflow* problem, not the *capability* problem. The underlying model isn't the differentiator — the product around it is.

---

## What Makes It Different From Simply Using ChatGPT or Gemini

| Generic AI Chat | LeetWrite AI |
|---|---|
| Requires writing a good prompt yourself | Prompt engineering is built-in and invisible |
| Output format varies run to run | Fixed, predictable, publish-ready structure every time |
| You copy/paste between multiple tools | One flow: paste, generate, copy |
| No guarantee of correct complexity analysis | Complexity is derived specifically from your code, every time |
| Generic, chatty tone | Written like a senior engineer's discussion post, consistently |
| Every session starts from zero | Purpose-built UI designed around this exact task |

A general-purpose chat model is a blank canvas. LeetWrite AI is a purpose-built instrument. The value isn't "AI can do this" — everyone knows that already. The value is "this specific tool does it faster, more consistently, and better formatted than you would get rebuilding the prompt yourself every time."

---

## Principles That Should Guide Future Development

1. **Never add friction to save a feature.** If a new capability requires an extra click, field, or decision from the user, it needs to earn that cost clearly.
2. **Stay narrow before going wide.** Every new surface (extension, integrations, accounts) should be added only once the core loop is excellent — not to appear feature-complete.
3. **The product should always feel faster than writing it yourself would.** The moment LeetWrite AI feels slower or more effortful than manually writing a discussion, the product has failed its core premise.
4. **Trust is earned through consistency, not promises.** Users should never have to double-check the AI's complexity analysis or wonder if the output is reliable.
5. **Design quality is not optional polish — it is the product.** In a category where the underlying AI capability is commoditized, product craft is the entire moat.

## What Kind of Company and Product This Aims to Become

Over the next five years, LeetWrite AI aims to become the default writing layer for technical growth — starting with LeetCode, expanding to the broader practice of explaining and documenting one's own technical work.

The trajectory:

- **Year 1:** The fastest, most trusted way to turn a LeetCode solution into a great discussion post. Zero setup, instant value.
- **Years 2–3:** A personal writing companion — one that learns a user's voice, lives where they already work (browser extension, editor), and extends from single problems to broader technical writing (PRs, blog posts, interview prep notes).
- **Years 4–5:** A category-defining product for "explain your work" — not tied to any single platform, used by engineers, students, and teams who care about communicating technical thinking clearly, not just producing it.

The company behind LeetWrite AI should look and operate like the products it draws inspiration from: small, deliberate, obsessed with craft, shipping fewer things at a much higher bar than the market average. Growth is a byproduct of trust, not the other way around.
